1. What the tool does — and its limits

The tool reads text you provide and (a) detects case references, statutes and constitutional Articles; (b) resolves a curated index of landmark Indian Supreme Court judgments to their citation; (c) flags cases in that index with known adverse treatment (e.g. overruled or doubted); and (d) links each item to a search on a third-party database for verification.

The index is not comprehensive. It covers a limited set of landmark constitutional judgments. Any case outside it is flagged needs lookup and is not resolved. Statute and Article detection is rule-based and pattern-matched; it does not confirm a provision is in force. Treatment flags are general guidance, are not exhaustive, and are not a substitute for checking the current status of a judgment ("noting up" / shepardising).
